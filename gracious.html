These stories in The Gracious Afterward: Delta Stories, are of a bygone era, voices from the deep beginning, and are fiction, informed though in very specific and detailed ways by one who lived the times. The period spanned is approximately 1914-1922. The place: Mississippi, Washington and Sunflower Counties, and the American South.

About the author:

Irv Broughton is a widely recognized chronicler of American life and culture on film, tape and in book forms. Eight of his sixteen books encompass the oral tradition: notable interviews with old-time pilots, WWII fliers, female pioneer pilots, American writers and poets, many early film and television producers and, finally, old-time Floridians.

Born in Orlando, Florida, he holds B.A. and M.A. degrees from Florida State University and an M.A. from the distinguished writing program at Hollins University. Irv has lived in Florida, Massachusetts, Virginia and Washington State, where he now resides.

An award-winning filmmaker, his films have shown at the Ann Arbor Film Festival, Bumbershoot, among others, and have won the Northwest Film and Video Festival three times. He has done documentaries on topics as diverse as Hmong needlework, the closing of  a Native American bar, an old mining town, as well as projects on writers. A major focus of his life’s work, to film an older generation of American writers, which he started in 1971, continues.

Irv sums up his ambitious attempt to remember what is so easily lost by saying, “I consider myself just a beneficiary of people’s telling—the gift of a nation’s stories.”
